Gothenberg : Sweden's Little London

Sweden's chief seaport and second largest city. It is the birthplace of Volvo Cars and home to Volvo museum.

Introduction :

Gothenberg lies along the Göta River estuary, about 5 miles (8 km) above that river's mouth in the Kattegat. Gothenburg is the principal city on Sweden's southwest coast and lies about 240 miles (390 km) southwest of Stockholm.

Climate :
  • The warm season lasts for 3.3 months, from May 30 to September 9, with an average daily high temperature above 62°F.
  • The hottest month of the year in Göteborg is July, with an average high of 68°F and low of 55°F.
  • The cold season lasts for 3.9 months, from November 22 to March 19, with an average daily high temperature below 42°F.
  • The coldest month of the year in Göteborg is February, with an average low of 26°F and high of 35°F.

Tourist Attractions :

  • Volvo Museum

    The Volvo Museum in Gothenburg, Sweden, covers the development of Sweden's leading vehicle manufacturer Volvo. From the first ÖV 4 to the current cars, trucks, buses and other products. The museum also has displays of Volvo Aero and Volvo Penta products, and many other exhibits, including the joint desk of Assar Gabrielsson and Gustaf Larson from the pioneering years of the company.

  • Museum of Fine Arts

    The Gothenburg Museum of Art has one of the foremost art collections in Northern Europe. Works by masters such as Rembrandt, Picasso and van Gogh are shown side by side with works by contemporary artists such as Charlotte Gyllenhammar, Lina Selander and Cajsa von Zeipel.

  • Göteborg Opera(GöteborgsOperan)

    GöteborgsOperan is renowned for its world-class performances, showcasing the talents of renowned singers, musicians, and dancers from around the globe. The opera house hosts a diverse range of productions, including classic operas, contemporary works, and innovative collaborations.

  • Skansen Kronan

    Historical building in the heart of Gothenburg with a magnificent view of the city. Between May - August, we have a large outdoor Terrace with grilled food, salads, classic shrimp sandwich, cold beer, sparkling wine, warm and cold coffee, ice-cream and Swedish home made cakes (Swedish fika).

Travel Tips :

  • The best time to visit Gothenburg is May-August.In summer, the temperature in Gothenburg can rise as high as 23℃ (74℉).
  • Local residents are self-sufficient and can stand up for themselves, being known for their straightforwardness. At the same time, they will never say anything that can offend a person.
  • Stick to well-lit areas and main streets. Don't carry large amounts of cash. Don't leave your belongings unattended: Keep your belongings close to you at all times and never leave them unattended.
